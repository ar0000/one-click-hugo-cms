Agriculture remains the backbone of Bangladesh's economy, employing a significant portion of the population and contributing critically to food security. Yet traditional farming practices face mounting pressure from climate change, land scarcity, and labor shortages.

## What is Smart Farming?

Smart farming — also known as precision agriculture — integrates digital technologies, sensor systems, data analytics, and automation to optimize every stage of the agricultural process. The result is higher yields, reduced input costs, and more sustainable land use.

Key technologies include:

- IoT-based soil and moisture sensors
- UAV (drone) crop monitoring and aerial mapping
- GIS-based land analysis and spatial planning
- Automated irrigation systems
- Greenhouse and hydroponic growing systems
